Conservation Programs

Join our global initiatives to protect endangered species and their habitats.

Making a Difference Together

Komodo Hub's conservation programs connect schools, communities, and individuals with real-world conservation efforts around the globe. By participating in these programs, you contribute directly to protecting endangered species and their habitats while gaining valuable knowledge and skills.

Each program is designed with specific conservation goals, educational components, and opportunities for hands-on involvement. Whether you're a school looking to engage students in meaningful environmental education, a community seeking to make a local impact, or an individual passionate about conservation, there's a program for you.

Coral Reef Restoration in Thailand

A network of schools in Thailand has successfully restored over 5 acres of damaged coral reef through our Marine Conservation program. Students monitored water quality, planted coral fragments, and created awareness campaigns that led to reduced pollution in the area.

Urban Wildlife Corridor in Toronto

A community group in Toronto transformed abandoned urban spaces into a wildlife corridor connecting two parks. The project has documented a 40% increase in local bird species and created habitat for pollinators and small mammals.

From Classroom to Conservation: Westlake Elementary's Journey

When Westlake Elementary School in California joined Komodo Hub, they were looking for ways to make environmental education more engaging. Three years later, they've transformed their campus into a certified wildlife habitat, documented 45 local bird species, and inspired their entire community to take conservation action.

Read Full Story

Community Power: How River Keepers Restored Local Watershed

The River Keepers community group started with just 10 members concerned about pollution in their local river. Using Komodo Hub's resources and network, they've grown to over 100 members, removed 15 tons of waste from the watershed, and successfully advocated for stronger local environmental protections.
