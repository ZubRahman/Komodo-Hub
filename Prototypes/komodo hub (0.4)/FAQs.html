Frequently Asked Questions

What is Komodo Hub?

Komodo Hub is a digital platform designed to support conservation efforts for endangered species in Indonesia. It provides education, knowledge-sharing, and opportunities for community involvement. By joining our community, you can stay updated on conservation projects, wildlife protection, and local initiatives.

How can I donate?

You can donate through our website by choosing a one-time or monthly donation amount. Simply select an option and confirm your donation. We accept payments via credit card, PayPal, and other secure methods. Your donation directly helps protect wildlife and support conservation programs.

Where does my donation go?

All donations are used to provide food, shelter, and medical care for rescued animals. Funds also support conservation programs, community education initiatives, and awareness campaigns aimed at protecting endangered species. Here are some of the key areas your donation supports:

  • Wildlife rescue and rehabilitation programs
  • Conservation fieldwork and research
  • Local community engagement and education
  • Protection of natural habitats
Can I contribute in ways other than donating money?

Yes! You can help by volunteering, raising awareness, and supporting local initiatives. We also welcome people to submit species records, participate in conservation activities, and join events focused on wildlife protection.

Can I sponsor an animal?

Yes! You can sponsor an animal by making a specific donation that supports the care and rehabilitation of a particular species. By sponsoring, you will receive updates on the animal's progress and how your contribution is making a difference in its life.

How do I know which animals need sponsorship?

We regularly update our website with information on animals that need sponsorship. You can view their profiles, learn about their story, and select the species you wish to support. Each animal's profile includes a detailed description of its rehabilitation needs and progress.

What happens to the animals after they are rehabilitated?

Once the animals are fully rehabilitated, they are carefully reintroduced to their natural habitat, if possible. In some cases, animals may be placed in sanctuaries where they can live freely in a protected environment. We continue to monitor their well-being and provide additional support as needed.
